Samosa Recipe

Description

Veg samosas are one of the most popular South Asian snacks, known for their crisp, golden pastry shell and flavorful spiced vegetable filling. The dish begins with a dough made from all-purpose flour, oil or ghee, and water, which is kneaded into a firm texture perfect for frying. The filling is usually a mixture of boiled potatoes, peas, onions, and green chilies, seasoned with aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, and chili powder. Together, the flaky crust and savory filling create a satisfying balance of textures and flavors that make samosas beloved across India, Nepal, and beyond.

Once the filling and dough are prepared, small portions of dough are rolled out, shaped into cones, and stuffed with the spiced vegetable mixture. The cones are sealed and deep-fried slowly over medium heat until crisp and golden brown. Samosas are often enjoyed hot, served with tangy tamarind chutney, spicy green chutney, or even tomato achar. Whether eaten as a street food snack, an appetizer, or part of a festive meal, veg samosas are a timeless comfort food that bring together warmth, crunch, and spice in every bite.

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • ½ tsp carom seeds (optional, for flavor)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Water (enough to knead a firm dough)

For the Filling

  • 3–4 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • ½ cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 small onion (optional, finely chopped)
  • 1–2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp oil (for cooking filling)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (2–3 tbsp)
  • Lemon juice, to taste

Steps

Preparing the dough

  1. In a large bowl, mix 2 cups all-purpose flour with ½ tsp carom seeds (optional), ½ tsp salt, and 3 tbsp oil or ghee.
  2. Rub the oil into the flour until the mixture feels crumbly.
  3. Gradually add water and knead into a firm, smooth dough.
  4. Cover and let it rest for 30–40 minutes.

Preparing the Filling

  1. Boil 3–4 medium potatoes until tender, then peel and roughly mash them.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan and add 1 tsp cumin seeds
  3. Sauté chopped ginger, green chilies, and optionally peas or carrots.
  4. Add spices: 1 tsp coriander powder, 1 tsp cumin powder, ½ tsp garam masala, ½ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp chili powder, and salt to taste.
  5. Stir in the mashed potatoes and mix well until evenly spiced.
  6. Finish with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice. Let the filling cool.

Shaping and frying the Samosas

  1. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each into a thin oval.
  2. Cut the oval in half to form two semi-circles.
  3. Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edge with water.
  4. Fill the cone with the potato mixture, then seal the top by pinching and folding.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-low heat.
  6. Fry samosas in batches, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crisp.
  7. Remove and drain on paper towels.

Serving

  1. Serve hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or spicy tomato achar.
