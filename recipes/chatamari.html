Chatamari Recipe

Description

Buff chatamari, often called the "Nepali pizza," is a traditional Newar dish made with a rice flour base and topped with spiced buffalo meat. The recipe begins by preparing a smooth batter from rice flour and water, which is then spread thinly onto a heated, lightly greased pan to form a crepe-like base. As the batter cooks, it firms up into a soft yet slightly crispy layer. The topping is prepared separately by mixing minced buffalo meat with chopped onions, garlic, ginger, green chilies, coriander, and spices such as cumin, coriander powder, and turmeric, creating a fragrant and savory mixture.

Once the rice crepe is half-cooked, the seasoned buffalo topping is spread evenly across the surface, often with a beaten egg cracked on top for added richness. The chatamari is then covered and cooked until the meat is fully done and the base develops a light golden edge. Served hot, buff chatamari is enjoyed as a flavorful snack or meal, offering a unique balance of textures between the tender rice crepe and the spiced meat topping, making it a cherished part of Newar cuisine.

Ingredients

For the base

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 2–2.5 cups water (adjust for a smooth, pourable batter)
  • Pinch of salt (optional)
  • A little oil or ghee (for greasing the pan)

For the Topping

  • 250–300g minced buffalo meat
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1–inch piece ginger, minced or grated
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped (2$ndash;3 tbsp)
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1–2 tbsp oil (mustard or sunflower oil is common)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1$ndash;2 eggs (optional, for cracking on top)

Steps

Preparing the batter

  1. In a bowl, mix rice flour and a pinch of salt.
  2. Gradually add water and whisk until you get a smooth, lump-free, pourable batter (similar to crepe batter).
  3. Set aside to rest for 10–15 minutes.

Preparing the Topping

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine minced buffalo meat, onion, garlic, ginger, chilies, and coriander leaves.
  2. Add cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric, oil, and salt.
  3. Mix well until the meat and spices are evenly combined.

Cooking the Chatamari

  1. Heat a flat non-stick pan or skillet and lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  2. Pour a ladle of rice batter onto the pan and spread it thinly in a circular motion to form a crepe-like base.
  3. (Optional) Crack an egg on top and spread it gently over the meat.
  4. Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low-medium heat for 5–7 minutes, until the meat is fully cooked and the base edges turn lightly golden.

Serving

  1. Slide the chatamari onto a plate and serve hot, cut into wedges if desired.
